# Loom walkthrough script (~4–5 min)

A tight script for the mandatory Loom. Aim for showing the core loop working
end-to-end, not narrating every file.

## 0. Setup before recording (30 sec, off-camera)
- `npm run dev` running, `ngrok http 4000` running, `.env` filled.
- Browser at http://localhost:5173, logged out. Have a headset/mic ready.
- Optional second tab: the code, open to `server/src/interview/engine.ts`.

## 1. The problem & approach (45 sec)
> "The brief asks for a mock interview that feels like a real conversation — the AI
> has to respond to what you actually said, follow up, push back, and know when to
> move on. So the two hard parts are: a genuinely dynamic conversation engine, and a
> clean real-time voice loop. Here's how I solved both."

## 2. Architecture, fast (45 sec) — show the README diagram
> "Voice is handled by Vapi in **custom-LLM mode** — it does speech-to-text, text-to-
> speech, and turn-taking, but every reply is generated by my own backend. So the
> intelligence is 100% mine. The brain is a **LangGraph state machine**: ONE streamed
> Gemini call both scores your last answer and speaks the next line — the score comes
> back as a hidden trailer behind the speech — then the graph branches on that
> decision: probe a weak answer, advance and raise difficulty on a strong one, or
> close when coverage is enough. One round-trip per answer, so it feels instant."
- Briefly show the graph in `engine.ts` (the `addConditionalEdges` block).

## 3. The core loop, live (2 min) — THE money shot
- Sign up (or log in) → pick **Behavioral** → **Begin interview**.
- Let Maya open and ask the first question. Answer it **deliberately vaguely**
  (e.g. "I worked on a project and it went well").
  > "Watch — I gave a vague answer on purpose."
- **Point out the follow-up:** the AI probes for specifics instead of moving on.
- Answer the follow-up with a concrete, strong answer (numbers, your role).
  > "Now a specific answer — notice it acknowledges the detail and moves to a new,
  > harder area." (this is the difficulty ratchet + acknowledgement)
- Show the **live transcript** updating and the interviewer state (speaking/listening).
- Interrupt/talk over it once to show barge-in feels natural.
- Click **End interview**.

## 4. The feedback report (45 sec)
- Show the generated report: overall score, per-dimension bars, and especially the
  **strengths/improvements that quote you verbatim**.
  > "The report is grounded in what I actually said — here's a direct quote it pulled."
- Back to **Dashboard**, show the session saved with its score.

## 5. Cost & close (30 sec)
- Flash COST.md.
> "Cost is ~$1–2 per interview, and the LLM brain is essentially free — I tier
> Gemini models (Flash-Lite to score, Flash to talk, a bigger model once for the
> report), disable thinking in the latency path, and lean on implicit prompt
> caching, so the whole brain runs on the free tier for a demo and ~2 cents at paid
> rates. Voice transport is the real cost, so I cap interview length. That's the
> whole loop: dynamic, voice-native, and grounded feedback."

## Tips
- The single most important thing to capture is the **unexpected follow-up on a vague
  answer** and the **acknowledgement of a strong one** — that's the bar the brief sets.
- Keep energy up; the loop is the star, not the code tour.
